## Overview
Steve Brunton is a Professor of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Washington, known for his exceptional YouTube channel (@Eigensteve) where he teaches applied mathematics, data science, and machine learning. He makes rigorous topics like probability, statistics, linear algebra, and control theory highly accessible through clear visual explanations. His two playlists — *Introduction to Statistics and Data Analysis* (35 videos) and *Probability Bootcamp* (44 videos) — form the core of this wiki's Prob/Stats study track.

## Key Facts
- **Position:** Professor, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, University of Washington
- **YouTube:** [@Eigensteve](https://www.youtube.com/@Eigensteve) — ~500K subscribers
- **Research areas:** Data-driven methods, dynamical systems, control theory, ML
- **Known collaborator:** J. Nathan Kutz (joint textbooks and courses)
- **Textbooks:** *Data-Driven Science and Engineering* (with Kutz) — [[prob-stats-plan]]
- **Teaching style:** Chalkboard/tablet derivations, strong physical intuition, connects theory to applications

## Role in Wiki
Primary educator for the **Prob/Stats track** (Apr 11 – May 9, 2026). His two playlists cover all foundational probability and statistics needed for FAANG ML system design interviews and GenAI engineering work. Directly relevant to: sampling distributions, Bayesian inference, MLE, hypothesis testing, CLT — all of which appear in ML interviews and underlie LLM behavior (temperature, sampling, token probabilities).
